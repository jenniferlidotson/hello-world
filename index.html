Surfing was born in Hawaiʻi and is deeply intertwined with its history and culture. If you dream of riding the waves, there’s no better place to learn how to surf and truly commune with the ocean. Learn about the origins of surfing in Hawaiʻi and find out where you can watch the pros or take a surfing lesson on your visit.

Stand Up Paddle Boarding (or SUP) came about in the 1960s when Hawaiian surfers began standing on their longboards and using outrigger canoe paddles to venture out for a better view of the surf. SUP is a popular choice because it’s great fun, has a relatively easy learning curve and offers a spectacular and close-up view of enchanting marine life; and it is a safe activity for people of all ages, sizes, and athletic abilities.
